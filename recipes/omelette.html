French-style omelette

Making an omelette is a delightful and versatile culinary skill. Whether you prefer a classic French-style omelette or an American-style version, here are the key steps to create a delicious omelette:

ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • milk

steps

  1. Beat the Eggs: Use two or three eggs per omelette, depending on your hunger level. Beat the eggs lightly with a fork then add same milk.
  2. Melt the Butter: Use an 8-inch nonstick skillet for a 2-egg omelette or a 9-inch skillet for 3 eggs. Melt the butter over medium-low heat, ensuring the temperature remains low and slow to prevent overcooking.
  3. Add the Eggs: Let the beaten eggs sit for a minute. Use a heatproof silicone spatula to gently lift the cooked eggs from the edges of the pan. Tilt the pan to allow the uncooked eggs to flow to the edge.
  4. Fill the Omelette: Add your desired fillings (but donâ€™t overstuff). Cook for a few more seconds until the eggs set.
  5. Fold and Serve: Fold the omelette in half. Slide it onto a plate using a silicone spatula.
